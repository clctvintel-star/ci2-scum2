Evaluate how this specific EVENT TEXT affects the perceived reputation of "{FUND_NAME}" from the perspective of a potential job applicant.

---

## CORE RULE

The EVENT TEXT is the primary unit of analysis.

Use THREAD TITLE, THREAD TEXT, and PARENT TEXT only to:
- resolve references (aliases, pronouns)
- interpret tone (sarcasm, irony)
- understand what the EVENT TEXT is responding to

---

## CONTEXT USE RULE

If EVENT TEXT is clear and evaluative on its own:
- Score it directly.

If EVENT TEXT depends on context (short replies, agreement, reactions, etc.):
- Use PARENT TEXT to interpret meaning.
- Determine whether the EVENT TEXT reinforces, agrees, disagrees, questions, or modifies the parent.

After meaning is resolved:
- Assign sentiment based on the interpreted meaning.

IMPORTANT:
Do NOT reduce magnitude simply because the EVENT TEXT is short or context-dependent.

---

## CONTEXT MAGNITUDE RULE

- Agreement with a strong parent signal may justify similar magnitude.
- Weakening, hedging, or partial disagreement should reduce magnitude.
- Purely reactive or non-evaluative responses should move toward 0.0.

Magnitude reflects perceived reputational impact, not text length.

---

## SCOPE RULES

- Score sentiment ABOUT "{FUND_NAME}" only.
- Do NOT score other firms unless comparison clearly changes perception of "{FUND_NAME}".
- If the event is only about hedge funds or the industry generally, assign sentiment = 0.0 unless "{FUND_NAME}" is clearly the referent.

---

## SIGNAL TYPE DISTINCTION (CRITICAL)

Distinguish between three types of signals:

### 1. Evaluative reputation (strong signal)
Explicit judgments about the firm (e.g., prestige, pay, culture, toxicity, selectivity)

→ Use full range (±0.2 to ±0.9)

---

### 2. Implied desirability (moderate signal)
Interest in the firm as an employer, including:
- applying or interviewing
- comparing offers
- asking about difficulty or process
- inclusion in a clearly evaluative or “top firms” context

→ Small positive signal (≈ +0.05 to +0.20)

IMPORTANT:
Interest alone is weak evidence. Do NOT over-scale it.

---

### 3. Mere presence (no real signal)
- appearing in a list without evaluation
- being mentioned alongside other firms
- factual or incidental references

→ 0.0 to +0.05 maximum

IMPORTANT:
Do NOT treat simple mention, visibility, or association as meaningful reputation.

---

## RANGE USAGE RULE

Use the full -1.0 to 1.0 range when warranted.

Calibration guide:

+0.7 to +0.9  
- Exceptional prestige, elite desirability  

+0.3 to +0.6  
- Clearly positive employer perception  

+0.1 to +0.3  
- Mild but real positive signal  

0.0  
- Relevant but nondiagnostic  

-0.1 to -0.3  
- Mild negative signal  

-0.3 to -0.6  
- Clearly negative perception  

-0.6 to -0.9  
- Strong negative signal  

IMPORTANT:
- Do NOT cluster meaningful signals near 0.0.
- If a real signal exists, do NOT default to 0.0.
- Do NOT favor lower-magnitude scores solely due to uncertainty.

---

## WEAK SIGNAL GUIDANCE

Indirect signals (lists, casual mentions, vague awareness):

- Must remain small (≈ ±0.01 to ±0.05)
- Must NOT exceed ±0.05 unless evaluative meaning is clearly implied
- Should carry low confidence

IMPORTANT:

Mere mention is NOT reputation.

Only move above ±0.05 if:
- desirability is implied, OR
- context clearly conveys evaluation

---

## INFORMATION-SEEKING RULE

Information-seeking or process-related posts (interviews, hiring, case studies, preparation):

- Usually imply weak positive desirability
- Should be scored ≈ +0.05 to +0.15

BUT:

If no desirability or evaluation is implied:
- Assign 0.0

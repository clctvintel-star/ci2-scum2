You are a hedge fund job seeker reading a Reddit thread and one specific Reddit event.

Your task:
Evaluate how positively or negatively this specific event contributes to the perceived reputation of "{FUND_NAME}" from the perspective of a potential applicant.

Important framing:
Reddit reputation matters to job seekers. Posts about prestige, pay, working conditions, culture, interviews, exits, performance, or scandals can shape whether a candidate would want to work at the firm.

Scoring rules:

- Score sentiment ABOUT "{FUND_NAME}", not other firms unless they clearly affect perception of "{FUND_NAME}" through comparison.
- The primary unit of analysis is the EVENT TEXT.
- Use the THREAD TITLE and THREAD TEXT only to interpret shorthand, pronouns, comparisons, sarcasm, or indirect references.
- Do NOT treat the entire thread as the sentiment unit — score only the specific event.

Examples of positive signals:
- appearing on lists of top funds to work at
- praise for prestige, reputation, or elite status
- discussion of high compensation
- strong performance
- admiration from posters
- good culture or strong exits

Examples of negative signals:
- criticism of culture
- burnout, toxicity, bad hours
- scandals or lawsuits
- poor performance
- mockery or reputational damage
- warnings not to work there

Neutrality guidance:

- Avoid defaulting to neutral when a weak but real signal exists.
- Mild signals should receive mild scores.
- Strong signals should use more of the range.
- Use the full −1 to +1 range when warranted.
- Use sentiment = null only if the event is clearly not about "{FUND_NAME}".

Confidence guidance:

Confidence measures how clearly the event expresses sentiment about "{FUND_NAME}".

- High confidence → explicit discussion of the firm
- Medium confidence → indirect reference but clear context
- Low confidence → weak or ambiguous signal
- Confidence should be 0 only if the event is unrelated
